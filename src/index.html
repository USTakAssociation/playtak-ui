          Playtak House Rules

          • Standard rules for Tak can be found here:
            • USTA Rules
            • Greater Than Games - How to play Tak [video]
          • White always moves first, though they place a Black stone on their first move.
          • Games may be played with komi, meaning that if the game ends in a flat count, black has a number of virtual flats added to their flat count, so that black may win or draw with fewer flats. The size of the komi is adjustable.
          • If neither player plays a stone or flattens a standing stone for 25 moves, the game is ruled a draw.

          Portable Tak Notation

          • PTN or Portable Tak Notation is a method of recording games and describing moves using text.
          • You can learn about the game notation here:
            • USTA - Portable Tak Notation
            • Simmon - Portable Tak Notation [video]

          Controls

          • Desktop
            • Left-click and drag to rotate the board.
            • Scroll wheel to zoom in and out.
            • Left-click a piece in your reserves to select it. Left-click on it again (or right-click) to rotate it to make it a wall.
            • Left-click a square on the board to place the selected piece or to pick up a piece or stack.
            • Right-click and hold on a stack/piece to hide pieces on all other squares (helps to see stack clearly).
          • Mobile
            • Touch and drag to rotate the board.
            • Two-finger pinch to zoom in and out
            • Tap a piece from your reserves to select it. Tap it again to rotate it to make it a wall.
            • Tap on a square on the board to place the selected piece or to pick up a piece or stack
          • General Notes
            • If any of the above controls don't work, it means that the control is invalid for the current move.
            • Current player to move is indicated by highlighting their name in the left pane.
            • Selecting the undo move in the left pane will require your opponent to also click it to approve it.
            • Selecting the offer draw in the left pane will require your opponent to also click it to accept it.

          Play Locally

          • From the login page select "Play Scratch"
          • Select "New Game" and navigate to the "Play Scratch" tab. From there, select your board size and hit "Play Scratch"

          Play Online

          • Login to server by clicking "Login" button, signup and then login.
          • Click on "New Game" in the menu to start a new game or accept another player's seek by selecting it in "Join game" menu.
          • Click on one of any ongoing games from "Watch" menu to watch an ongoing game.

          Board Settings

          • Click on the "Settings" Icon to change the board style, piece styles and other options.
          • You can set a board overlay to customize the look of the board. You can download the template here. Board Overlay Template
          • You can also change the color and the texture of the border, the border texture can use any image but may need to be adjusted to fit correctly.
